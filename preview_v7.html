Iconic Identity
What Is This Document?
Your complete brand system — from first impression to final word.

This is the Tommy Mello Iconic Identity.

It is not a style guide. It is not a logo sheet. It is the operating manual for the brand — defining exactly how Tommy Mello looks, sounds, and shows up at every stage of the business journey.

Every tool in this document exists to do one thing: make the brand consistent, credible, and impossible to misread.

Whether you're building a content calendar, designing an event stage, writing an email sequence, or publishing a podcast episode — this document tells you how it's done right.

Use it as a reference. Enforce it as a standard. Build with it as a system.

"Structure creates freedom."

Guidelines
Typography
Guidelines

Typography is a key element in branding. Your typefaces reflect the brand's character. Use them consistently across all materials — website, social, print — to maintain a cohesive look.

Consistent use of line spacing, letter spacing, and margins enhances readability and creates a clean, professional appearance.

Headlines & Section Labels

Always set in Inter 700-900. Use all caps or title case for major headlines. Color: Azure Blue (#018ABE), Midnight Navy (#001B48), or white on dark backgrounds.

Accent Labels & CTAs

Josefin Sans Bold for section labels, CTAs, keyword tags, and condensed accent moments. Always in uppercase with generous letter-spacing.

Body Copy

Inter Regular at 18-20px. Line height: 1.65-1.75 minimum. Never set body copy in all caps. Keep column width to 60-75 characters per line for readability.

Pull Quotes & Italics

Inter Italic 400 or 300 for pull quotes and editorial captions. Provides warmth and contrast against bold display text. Size: 20-28px.

Hierarchy Rule

Every page has one dominant typographic element. Two supporting elements maximum. No competing weights at the same visual level.

Art Direction
Photography
Direction
LIGHTING

Dramatic and directional. Stage-quality light — backlighting, spotlights, strong contrast between subject and environment. Shadows are compositional assets, not problems to fix.

SETTING

Stage and event venues. High-end private offices. Podcast studios with intentional lighting. Urban architecture with strong geometry. No generic conference rooms. No beige backgrounds.

WARDROBE

Dark, fitted, deliberate. Business casual to formal — navy, charcoal, black. No loud patterns. Tommy looks like an operator who earned the room, not someone renting it for the afternoon.

EDITING STYLE

Deep contrast. Slight cool-blue grade in shadows. Minimal retouching. Tonal range compressed into brand palette — deep navy in darks, azure in mid-tones, bronze warmth in highlights.

SUBJECT ENERGY

Commanding but not rigid. In motion or in thought. Never posed like a headshot. Always looks like something is happening — a decision being made, an audience being led.

Brand Archetypes
The Tommy Mello
Brand Archetypes

Brand archetypes are the universal patterns of character that define how a brand thinks, speaks, and shows up in the world. They create instant recognition because they tap into how people naturally understand character and trust.

The Tommy Mello brand operates across four archetypes — each representing a distinct layer of how Tommy leads, teaches, and connects. Together, they form the complete brand personality.

01/ THE CITIZEN— The Blue-Collar Leader

Belong, relate, and prove people like me can win.

02/ THE HERO— The Builder & Scaler

Prove capability through discipline, effort, and results.

03/ THE SAGE— The Systems Thinker

Understand truth and turn complexity into clarity.

04/ THE RULER— The Empire Builder

Build something that lasts, scales, and leads others.

Brand Tone
"A high-level operator explaining exactly what works, in plain English, without wasting your time."

Tommy speaks like someone who has seen how things actually play out, isn't trying to impress you, and isn't trying to soften the truth. There's no performance in it.

CONFIDENT

ACCOUNTABLE
